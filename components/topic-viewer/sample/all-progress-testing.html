Supporting California Native Americans

Through Executive Order, Governor Newsom issued the state's first apology to California Native Americans for the violence, maltreatment and neglect California inflicted, and created a Truth and Healing Council. The Governor established a first-ever California Cabinet-level Office of Tribal Affairs and appointed the first Tribal Affairs Secretary. Governor Newsom also encouraged Administration-wide action to support tribal access, co-management, and ownership of their historically dispossessed lands.

Unprecedented support for California's immigrant communities

As the nation’s most diverse state, California is stronger and more vibrant because of our immigrant communities. California has led efforts to mitigate the harm caused by federal inaction through policies that benefit our communities and the economy. Governor Newsom has:
  • Expanded immigrant access to state IDs and vital government programs providing health care, food, financial relief, legal assistance, and education regardless of status.
  • Ended the Trump Administration’s policy to use California National Guard soldiers for immigration enforcement in 2019, and redeployed those units to support wildfire prevention.
  • Created a new Department of Social Services Office of Immigrant Youth to coordinate supportive programs for immigrant youth, invested in school-based supports, and supported ongoing funding for free legal representation for unaccompanied minors.
  • Advanced efforts to unleash immigrant talent by funding entrepreneurship training and microgrants for limited English proficient Californians and undocumented Californians, including DACA beneficiaries and signing legislation to allow expedited professional licensing for refugees, asylees, and special immigrant visa holders.
  • Provided ongoing funding for pro-bono immigration legal services.
  • Created a Rapid Response Fund to support critical assistance/services to immigrants. Removed the word “alien” from use in state law.
  • Announced a new farmworker immigration-related legal services pilot program to start in early 2024.
  • Advanced efforts to support work permits for undocumented farmworkers by signing legislation authorizing the state to enter into an agreement with the federal government.

Conserving California's natural beauty

The Governor established the nation’s first 30 by 30 conservation goal – 30% percent of the state’s lands and coastal waters preserved by 2030 – with 24.4% of land and 16.2% water conserved so far. Governor Newsom also supported the Klamath Dams Removal, the largest river restoration project in American history. At the same time, the Newsom Administration has expanded outdoor and state parks access for all Californians through Outdoors for All programs like the Adventure and Golden Bear Passes.

Cleaner, more reliable power

Governor Newsom is investing billions and accelerating clean energy projects through permitting, planning, and procurement reforms to meet ambitious clean electricity targets of 90% by 2035, 95% by 2040, and 100% by 2045. To support California's clean energy future, the Governor has advanced sustainable lithium production in Lithium Valley that will meet global demand for this essential element used in batteries while providing Californians a share of the benefits.

Ensuring safe and affordable drinking water

Governor Newsom created the Safe and Affordable Drinking Water Fund, which has improved access to safe drinking water for over 2 million Californians. In addition, the Governor has invested $7.3 billion over five years for immediate drought and flood response to ensure long-term water resilience and championed the Delta Conveyance Project to allow the state to capture, move, and store water.

Keeping communities safer from wildfire

As California’s fire seasons get longer and more intense, Governor Newsom’s Administration is rising to meet the challenge. The Governor has embraced an “all of the above” wildfire strategy, including:
  • Increasing the pace and scale of forest management
  • Supporting and equipping our state’s firefighting crews
  • Modernizing wildfire response assets and infrastructure
Governor Newsom has invested $2.7 billion in forest management to make California’s forests and wildlands more resilient to wildfire -- backed by a Wildfire and Forest Resilience Action Plan with 100% of the 99 key actions outlined underway or completed. In addition, California has maintained the world’s largest aerial fleet, fast-tracked cutting-edge AI technology, secured access to Pentagon satellites for wildfire detection, and invested and deployed more than 1,000 infrared remote-controlled “lookout” cameras. Governor Newsom also established the state's first Wildfire Forecast and Threat Intelligence Center to provide real-time data, and serve as a central organizing hub for wildfire forecasting, weather information, threat intelligence gathering, and analysis.

Taking on Big Oil -- and Winning

Throughout his time in office, Governor Newsom has taken on Big Oil, including suing oil companies for their 50 years of deception and damage. He signed first-in-the-nation corporate climate disclosure bills and created an independent watchdog to hold oil companies accountable for gas price gouging.

The most ambitious climate action plan in the world

Governor Newsom is leading the global shift to our sustainable future -- more clean energy, less pollution. Governor Newsom's California Climate Commitment invests a record amount to achieve carbon neutrality by 2045, cut air pollution by 71%, and drop gas consumption by 94%, while creating 4 million new jobs, through critical climate solutions. Under the Newsom Administration, California is leading the global transition to zero emission vehicles, requiring all new cars sold to be zero-emission by 2035. The state surpassed 1.5 million ZEVs sold in California in 2023, two years ahead of schedule.

Budgets that invest in Californians and California values

Through strategic planning, the Governor’s balanced, responsible budgets support core programs and build reserves to serve our state’s most vulnerable populations. Governor Newsom's budgets have maintained billions in reserves, including a robust rainy day fund. The Newsom Administration has put $12.4 billion toward reducing pension liabilities over the last five years, with billions more in the coming years.

Generational investment in California's infrastructure

California is making the biggest investment in infrastructure since the 1950s and 60s. It’s a once-in-a-generation drive that will benefit communities across California. Under Governor Newsom's leadership, the state is investing $180 billion in infrastructure over the next 10 years. This money will fund upgrades that benefit every community – especially disadvantaged communities. Already, over $41 billion is at work in communities across the state that help build our 100% clean electric grid, ensure safe drinking water and boost the state’s water supply and modernize our transportation system. The Governor has signed legislation that accelerates construction timelines on the projects necessary to achieving the state’s ambitious climate and clean energy goals.

Growing California's economic dominance

California does business like nowhere else. From the innovation powerhouse of Silicon Valley to the verdant farms of the Central Valley to the entertainment capital of the world in Hollywood, business thrives in the Golden State. Under Governor Newsom, California has prioritized $1.6 billion for community-led economic and climate resilience efforts, including California Jobs First, committed to cutting red tape and streamlining the state’s notoriously cumbersome permitting processes, invested in and extended California’s Film and Television Tax Credit Program, and restored the research and development tax credit, keeping California and the U.S. at the forefront of innovation.

Investing in workers and families

Governor Newsom has:
  • Led a nearly $2 billion expansion of childcare slots and signed legislation that set the stage for family child care providers to unionize.
  • Expanded after-school and summer education programs.
  • Expanded the California Earned Income Tax Credit from $400 million in 2019 to over $1.1 billion beginning 2020, which included the creation of the Young Child Tax Credit, and expanded access to otherwise eligible ITIN filers in 2020. The expansion has resulted in over $5.2 billion in CalEITC and YCTC paid to Californians since 2019.
  • Invested in career pathways programs to train workers and connect them to good-paying jobs and formed a Council on Career Education via executive order.
  • Invested $6 billion in Broadband for All to increase broadband infrastructure, access, and affordability.

Protecting California workers

Throughout his time in office, Governor Newsom has signed nation-leading legislative measures to expand protections for workers, including by ending exploitative piece-rate compensation for garment industry workers, expanding union rights for farm workers, establishing nation-leading transparency measures for companies employing warehouse workers. Additionally, the Governor signed legislation guaranteeing workers at least five paid sick days per year, up from three days, and expanded paid family leave and wage replacement.

Protecting California consumers

Governor Newsom established the California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation to protect Californians against abusive and predatory financial products and services. To better protect consumers, the Governor has also signed payday lender protections into law, barring payday lenders from charging high interest rates –previously as high as 200 percent – on loans between $2,500 and $10,000. The Governor also signed legislation including a crackdown on for-profit colleges disguising themselves as non-profit institutions and the creation of a student bill of rights.

Relief for small businesses

The Governor ensured relief for small businesses in 2021 by cutting small business taxes by $6.2 billion, creating a nearly $4 billion small business COVID-19 relief program, investing in the CalCompetes program, and funding two rounds of the Main Street Small Business Hiring Tax Credits.

A government that looks more like California

Governor Newsom is deeply committed to building an administration that reflects California’s diversity at every level of state government. The Governor has made historic statewide appointments like California's first Latino U.S. Senator, Alex Padilla, and California's first Latina Justice on the California Supreme Court, Patricia Guerrero. Governor Newsom also signed legislation allowing Californians to serve on appointed government boards and commissions regardless of immigration status.

All Californians can access health care, regardless of immigration status

Governor Newsom has led the charge to expand health care access to all Californians, regardless of age, immigration status, or ability to pay. The Governor has expanded full-scope Medi-Cal to all income-eligible Californians, including the undocumented.

Better mental health care for all Californians

Governor Newsom signed legislation improving access to quality behavioral health services for all Californians, including homeless people. The Governor has invested $4.7 billion for the California Master Plan for Kids’ Mental Health to ensure California kids, parents and communities have increased access to mental health and substance use disorder treatment services. Additionally, the Governor is ensuring mental health is treated the same as physical health through mental health parity legislation, including enforcement actions against health insurers, and billions in investments to expand mental health care for low-income Californians.

Improving health care for California's most vulnerable

In addition to expanding access to Medi-Cal to all Californians, regardless of immigration status, Governor Newsom has championed CalAIM Medi-Cal reform to fundamentally reimagine health care delivery for those with the most acute needs. The Governor renewed the MCO tax to provide the largest Medi-Cal rate increase in California history, expanding low-income patients’ access to critical health care services. At the same time, the Governor has added new benefits to Medi-Cal, including doulas for birthing assistance; community health services like meals and housing; trauma/ACEs and disability screening for children; dementia screening for older adults; and enhanced care management for complex care.

Making health care more affordable for California families

Through Covered California, the state became the first in the nation to offer financial help for individual health insurance on top of federal support – including expanded subsidies to qualified middle class individuals. The Governor also announced the CalRx generic drug program, making California the first state to have its own generic drug label, and announced efforts to make insulin and Naloxone more affordable.

Protecting abortion access and rights

Governor Newsom has expanded and protected access to reproductive care:
  • Invested more than $200 million in reproductive care access, plus an additional $200 million for comprehensive family planning services.
  • Signed an executive order and legislation protecting Californians and others traveling to California for abortion care as well as California providers from anti-choice state actions, launched the Reproductive Freedom Alliance, a non-partisan coalition of 22 Governors, and took actions to protect medication abortion access.
  • Signed numerous bills expanding access and protections for reproductive health care, including measures to provide stronger protections for reproductive health providers, expand the reproductive health workforce, and protect patient privacy.

Pushing treatment, not tents

Governor Newsom is tackling California’s homelessness crisis head-on. No more excuses, no more passing the buck – California is finally getting serious about this decades-long emergency with the compassion and urgency it demands. The Newsom Administration has invested $2.2 billion through the Behavioral Health Continuum Infrastructure Program to build approximately 2,500 behavioral health beds and create thousands of treatment slots to serve the needs of Californians with mental health and substance use challenges. To better treat those experiencing crisis, the governor created the Mobile Crisis Response program, investing $1.4 billion over five years so that people experiencing behavioral health crises, receive coordinated, more timely behavioral health care through Medi-Cal. Additionally, Governor Newsom signed legislation to modernize California’s conservatorship laws for the first time in more than 50 years and created CARE Court, a new framework to provide care, services, and housing to individuals with the most serious, untreated mental health and substance use disorders. The Governor has also expanded funding for the treatment of substance use disorder by leveraging over $200 million in federal opioid-related funding to support the Medication Assisted Treatment Expansion Project.

Supporting older Californians

Governor Newsom developed the Master Plan for Aging, a 10-year blueprint to advance the longevity, wellbeing, and inclusion of people of all ages and abilities, as well as launched the Task Force on Alzheimer’s Disease Prevention and Preparedness, and invested in expanded health, home, and dementia care, as well as basic income and meals.

Building more housing, faster

Governor Newsom has invested over $40 billion to boost affordable housing across the state, including through expanded state tax credits, infrastructure grants, and funding for climate-friendly housing. In addition, the Governor launched a new Housing Accountability Unit to hold local governments accountable for implementing state housing laws, including a new, legally binding requirement that California’s cities and counties plan for 2.5 million new units by roughly 2030, 1 million of which must be affordable. The Governor has also signed 32 housing-related CEQA reforms since taking office to cut bureaucratic red tape and streamline building.

Tackling California's homelessness crisis head-on

No more excuses, no more passing the buck – California is finally getting serious about this decades-long emergency with the compassion and urgency it demands. The Governor has invested over $15.3 billion to address homelessness and over $40 billion to expand affordable housing – and, critically, he’s demanding unprecedented accountability from local governments to make sure these investments have the intended effects. The Governor has also pioneered nation-leading homeless housing reforms, including Homekey and Project Roomkey, which have brought more than 71,000 people off the streets. Additionally, California is investing $750 million in Encampment Resolution Funding to get people out of encampments and into housing, and is delivering up to 1,200 small homes to provide safe, interim housing for people experiencing homelessness, free of charge and ready for occupancy.

Combating gun violence

California’s strong gun laws work – our state is ranked #1 in gun safety, with a gun death rate 43% lower than the national average. And Governor Newsom is doing even more to get weapons of war off California streets:
  • Strengthened and expanded “red flag laws” and ghost gun regulations and prohibited the sale of semiautomatic rifles to people under 21.
  • Invested $10 million in local government efforts to seize weapons from people who are prohibited from having them and $5 million in a public awareness campaign about red flag laws.
  • Signed additional legislation to strengthen public carry regulations; require microstamping on handgun cartridges; generate $160 million annually on the sale of bullets to improve school safety and gun violence intervention programs; keep guns out of the hands of potentially dangerous individuals; update the definition of a firearm to include ghost gun parts; and strengthen the process for removing firearms from people who are prohibited from owning them.
  • Proposed a Right to Safety amendment to the United States Constitution to enshrine fundamental, broadly supported gun safety measures into law.
  • Signed a law to hold the gun industry accountable by allowing private citizens to sue those who manufacture, distribute, or sell unlawful assault weapons, ghost guns, and ghost gun kits, taking advantage of the door left open by the U.S. Supreme Court decision regarding Texas’s abortion law.

Cracking down on hate crimes

Under Governor Newsom's leadership, California has invested $150 million for Stop the Hate grants to support community services for victims and survivors of hate crimes and launched CA vs. Hate, a new statewide hotline to report hate acts in California. The Governor established the Commission on the State of Hate to assess data on hate crimes in California, provide resources for victims, and make policy recommendations to better protect civil rights. At the same time, Governor Newsom has expanded the state nonprofit security grant program to help bolster security at targeted religious and community-based nonprofits.

Fighting the fentanyl and opioid crisis

The Newsom Administration has stayed laser-focused on fighting the opioid crisis, particularly the scourge of fentanyl. Governor Newsom has invested $7.9 million in 2022-23 and $6.7 million ongoing in a new Fentanyl Enforcement Program and $30 million to expand the Military Department’s existing drug interdiction efforts. Over 62,000 pounds of fentanyl were seized by the California National Guard in 2023, a 116% increase in seizures since 2022. Additionally, California is cracking down on opioid use and preventing overdoses through $151 million for the Naloxone Distribution project, $61 million for prevention and harm reduction, $10 million for education, testing, recovery, and support services, $4 million to make more test strips available, and $3.5 million to provide overdose medication to all middle and high schools, and making naloxone in a nasal form a targeted drug for CalRx medication accessibility initiative.

Keeping Californians safe

Under Governor Newsom, California has made major investments in local law enforcement and prosecutors to combat violent crime. The Governor is deploying a Public Safety Plan backed by a record investment, including over $374 million to combat organized retail theft, $88 million for opioid interdiction and enforcement, and $197 million to address gun violence. Part of the Public Safety Plan includes $6 million in a multi-year recruitment campaign to fill 1,000 CHP vacancies.

Ramping up efforts to stop organized retail theft

Governor Newsom expanded CHP’s Organized Retail Crime Task Force, which has led to 1,246 investigations leading to 2,100 arrests and 596,319 stolen items recovered, valued at over $25 million.

Reforming California's criminal justice system

The Governor has placed a moratorium on the death penalty, closed unneeded prisons, and instituted laws on police use of force that are a model to the rest of the nation. In addition, the Governor signed legislation phasing out the use of all private, for-profit prisons and led the charge that ended juvenile incarceration at the state level.

Making college more affordable and accessible

Governor Newsom has invested over $40 billion to boost affordable housing across the state, including through expanded state tax credits, infrastructure grants, and funding for climate-friendly housing. In addition, the Governor launched a new Housing Accountability Unit to hold local governments accountable for implementing state housing laws, including a new, legally binding requirement that California’s cities and counties plan for 2.5 million new units by roughly 2030, 1 million of which must be affordable. The Governor has also signed 32 housing-related CEQA reforms since taking office to cut bureaucratic red tape and streamline building.

The greatest higher ed system in the world is getting even better

Governor Newsom provided up to two years of community college tuition-free. Additionally, the Governor has made major investments in college affordability, including $2.1 billion to build affordable student housing at UC, CSU, and the community colleges, $115 million for Open Educational Resources at the California Community Colleges, $636.2 million to reform and strengthen the Middle Class Scholarship program, and efforts to pursue zero-cost textbook courses and make the College Corps program permanent. Through College Corps, the Governor has created new debt-free pathways to college while engaging students across the state in solving problems in their communities by providing 10,000+ college students with opportunities to support and learn from community-based organizations and earn up to $10,000.

Transforming K-12 education with a parents agenda

Governor Newsom is putting parents first – giving them choices about how their kids are educated and expanding childcare and leave benefits to ensure their families are supported. The Governor has:
  • Added a whole new grade to public school for 4-year-olds.
  • Expanded after-school and summer education programs.
  • Invested $4.1 billion to help one out of every three schools in California transition to the community school model, with wraparound mental health, social, and family services. Invested $18.7 million for dyslexia research and screening tool pilot projects at the University of California and $1 million in 2023 to convene an independent panel of experts on dyslexia.
  • Invested $250 million in 2022 and 2023 in early literacy supports.
  • Invested $3.5 billion for teacher preparation.
  • Invested $1.9 billion in the CalKIDS program to set up career savings accounts of up to $1,500 for every vulnerable student.
  • Invested $250 million in the K-16 Education Collaboratives Grant Program to strengthen pathways to career opportunities and address long-standing equity gaps.
  • Created the Office of Cradle to Career Data, which has signed landmark legal agreements with 15 education, social service, and career pathways entities, allowing for the creation of longitudinal data sets on student progress.
  • Augmented special education funding by more than $3.5 billion over the past five years.
  • Signed legislation ending book bans in schools, prohibiting censorship of instructional materials, and strengthening California law requiring schools to provide all students access to textbooks that teach about California’s diverse communities.
