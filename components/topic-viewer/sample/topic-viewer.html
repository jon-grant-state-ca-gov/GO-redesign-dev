The most ambitious climate action plan in the world

Governor Newsom is leading the global shift to our sustainable future -- more clean energy, less pollution. Governor Newsom's California Climate Commitment invests a record amount to achieve carbon neutrality by 2045, cut air pollution by 71%, and drop gas consumption by 94%, while creating 4 million new jobs, through critical climate solutions. Under the Newsom Administration, California is leading the global transition to zero emission vehicles, requiring all new cars sold to be zero-emission by 2035. The state surpassed 1.5 million ZEVs sold in California in 2023, two years ahead of schedule.

Public safety

Keeping Californians Safe

Under Governor Newsom, California has made major investments in local law enforcement and prosecutors to combat violent crime. The Governor is deploying a Public Safety Plan backed by a record investment to:

  • combat organized retail theft
  • increase opioid enforcement
  • address gun violence

Part of the Public Safety Plan includes a multi-year recruitment campaign to fill 1,000 CHP vacancies.

Public safety

Fighting the fentanyl and opioid crisis

The Newsom Administration has stayed laser-focused on fighting the opioid crisis, particularly the scourge of fentanyl. Governor Newsom has invested $7.9 million in 2022-23 and $6.7 million ongoing in a new Fentanyl Enforcement Program and $30 million to expand the Military Department’s existing drug interdiction efforts. Over 62,000 pounds of fentanyl were seized by the California National Guard in 2023, a 116% increase in seizures since 2022. Additionally, California is cracking down on opioid use and preventing overdoses through $151 million for the Naloxone Distribution project, $61 million for prevention and harm reduction, $10 million for education, testing, recovery, and support services, $4 million to make more test strips available, and $3.5 million to provide overdose medication to all middle and high schools, and making naloxone in a nasal form a targeted drug for CalRx medication accessibility initiative.

Health care

Making health care more affordable for California families

Through Covered California, the state became the first in the nation to offer financial help for individual health insurance on top of federal support – including expanded subsidies to qualified middle class individuals. The Governor also announced the CalRx generic drug program, making California the first state to have its own generic drug label, and announced efforts to make insulin and Naloxone more affordable.

Public safety

Combating gun violence

California’s strong gun laws work – our state is ranked #1 in gun safety, with a gun death rate 43% lower than the national average. And Governor Newsom is doing even more to get weapons of war off California streets:

  • Strengthened and expanded “red flag laws” and ghost gun regulations and prohibited the sale of semiautomatic rifles to people under 21.
  • Invested $10 million in local government efforts to seize weapons from people who are prohibited from having them and $5 million in a public awareness campaign about red flag laws.
  • Signed additional legislation to strengthen public carry regulations; require microstamping on handgun cartridges; generate $160 million annually on the sale of bullets to improve school safety and gun violence intervention programs; keep guns out of the hands of potentially dangerous individuals; update the definition of a firearm to include ghost gun parts; and strengthen the process for removing firearms from people who are prohibited from owning them.
  • Proposed a Right to Safety amendment to the United States Constitution to enshrine fundamental, broadly supported gun safety measures into law.
  • Signed a law to hold the gun industry accountable by allowing private citizens to sue those who manufacture, distribute, or sell unlawful assault weapons, ghost guns, and ghost gun kits, taking advantage of the door left open by the U.S. Supreme Court decision regarding Texas’s abortion law.

Health care

Better mental health care for all Californians

Governor Newsom signed legislation improving access to quality behavioral health services for all Californians, including homeless people. The Governor has invested $4.7 billion for the California Master Plan for Kids’ Mental Health to ensure California kids, parents and communities have increased access to mental health and substance use disorder treatment services. Additionally, the Governor is ensuring mental health is treated the same as physical health through mental health parity legislation, including enforcement actions against health insurers, and billions in investments to expand mental health care for low-income Californians.

Health care

Pushing treatment, not tents

Governor Newsom is tackling California’s homelessness crisis head-on. No more excuses, no more passing the buck – California is finally getting serious about this decades-long emergency with the compassion and urgency it demands. The Newsom Administration has invested $2.2 billion through the Behavioral Health Continuum Infrastructure Program to build approximately 2,500 behavioral health beds and create thousands of treatment slots to serve the needs of Californians with mental health and substance use challenges. To better treat those experiencing crisis, the Governor created the Mobile Crisis Response program, investing $1.4 billion over five years so that people experiencing behavioral health crises, receive coordinated, more timely behavioral health care through Medi-Cal. Additionally, Governor Newsom signed legislation to modernize California’s conservatorship laws for the first time in more than 50 years and created CARE Court, a new framework to provide care, services, and housing to individuals with the most serious, untreated mental health and substance use disorders. The Governor has also expanded funding for the treatment of substance use disorder by leveraging over $200 million in federal opioid-related funding to support the Medication Assisted Treatment Expansion Project.

Economy

Investing in workers and families

Governor Newsom has:

  • Led a nearly $2 billion expansion of childcare slots and signed legislation that set the stage for family child care providers to unionize.
  • Expanded the California Earned Income Tax Credit from $400 million in 2019 to over $1.1 billion beginning 2020, which included the creation of the Young Child Tax Credit, and expanded access to otherwise eligible ITIN filers in 2020. The expansion has resulted in over $5.2 billion in CalEITC and YCTC paid to Californians since 2019.
  • Invested in career pathways programs to train workers and connect them to good-paying jobs and formed a Council on Career Education via executive order.
  • Invested $6 billion in Broadband for All to increase broadband infrastructure, access, and affordability.

Schools

Making college more affordable and accessible

Governor Newsom provided up to two years of community college tuition-free. Additionally, the Governor has made major investments in college affordability, including $2.1 billion to build affordable student housing at UC, CSU, and the community colleges, $115 million for Open Educational Resources at the California Community Colleges, $636.2 million to reform and strengthen the Middle Class Scholarship program, and efforts to pursue zero-cost textbook courses and make the College Corps program permanent. Through College Corps, the Governor has created new debt-free pathways to college while engaging students across the state in solving problems in their communities by providing 10,000+ college students with opportunities to support and learn from community-based organizations and earn up to $10,000.

Housing

Tackling California's homelessness crisis head-on

No more excuses, no more passing the buck – California is finally getting serious about this decades-long emergency with the compassion and urgency it demands. The Governor has invested over $15.3 billion to address homelessness and over $40 billion to expand affordable housing – and, critically, he’s demanding unprecedented accountability from local governments to make sure these investments have the intended effects. The Governor has also pioneered nation-leading homeless housing reforms, including Homekey and Project Roomkey, which have brought more than 71,000 people off the streets. Additionally, California is investing $750 million in Encampment Resolution Funding to get people out of encampments and into housing, and is delivering up to 1,200 small homes to provide safe, interim housing for people experiencing homelessness, free of charge and ready for occupancy.

Health care

Protecting abortion access and rights

Governor Newsom has expanded and protected access to reproductive care:

  • Invested more than $200 million in reproductive care access, plus an additional $200 million for comprehensive family planning services.
  • Signed an executive order and legislation protecting Californians and others traveling to California for abortion care as well as California providers from anti-choice state actions, launched the Reproductive Freedom Alliance, a non-partisan coalition of 22 Governors, and took actions to protect medication abortion access.
  • Signed numerous bills expanding access and protections for reproductive health care, including measures to provide stronger protections for reproductive health providers, expand the reproductive health workforce, and protect patient privacy.

Schools

Transforming K-12 education with a parents agenda

Governor Newsom is putting parents first – giving them choices about how their kids are educated and expanding childcare and leave benefits to ensure their families are supported. The Governor has:

  • Added a whole new grade to public school for 4-year-olds.
  • Expanded after-school and summer education programs.
  • Invested $4.1 billion to help one out of every three schools in California transition to the community school model, with wraparound mental health, social, and family services. Invested $18.7 million for dyslexia research and screening tool pilot projects at the University of California and $1 million in 2023 to convene an independent panel of experts on dyslexia.
  • Invested $250 million in 2022 and 2023 in early literacy supports.
  • Invested $3.5 billion for teacher preparation.
  • Invested $1.9 billion in the CalKIDS program to set up career savings accounts of up to $1,500 for every vulnerable student.
  • Invested $250 million in the K-16 Education Collaboratives Grant Program to strengthen pathways to career opportunities and address long-standing equity gaps.
  • Created the Office of Cradle to Career Data, which has signed landmark legal agreements with 15 education, social service, and career pathways entities, allowing for the creation of longitudinal data sets on student progress.
  • Augmented special education funding by more than $3.5 billion over the past five years.
  • Signed legislation ending book bans in schools, prohibiting censorship of instructional materials, and strengthening California law requiring schools to provide all students access to textbooks that teach about California’s diverse communities.
